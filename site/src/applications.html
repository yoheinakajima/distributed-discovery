Applications

The framework travels only when every stage of the pipeline has a defensible domain mapping.

Four precise translations

Science

Evidence is prior experimental and observational work; actions are new experiments or measurements; coverage is the set of hypotheses, mechanisms, or empirical regions those actions can distinguish. Duplicate experiments may still be valuable for replication, so overlap is not automatically waste.

Research & development

Evidence is technical and market learning; actions are funded prototypes or development paths; coverage is the opportunity or failure-mode space tested. The feasible action set includes capital, staffing, and technical dependencies.

Venture portfolios

Evidence is diligence and market belief; actions are investments with size and follow-on constraints; coverage is exposure to distinct hypotheses, technologies, or demand paths. Returns are not binary box hits, so atomic top-ranked selection is only a reference point.

Multi-agent AI

Evidence is model context, retrieval, tools, and shared memory; actions are investigations, proposals, or tool calls; coverage is the candidate or test space reached. Nominal agent count is not evidence diversity when provenance and model state are shared.

Boundary condition

A mapping is not a result

These translations identify objects to measure. They do not establish that the canonical ordering, top-portfolio rule, or equilibrium guarantees survive. Each application needs its own action technology, objective, dependence model, and evidence standard.
