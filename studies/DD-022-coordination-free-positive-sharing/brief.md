# Brief

DD-022 freezes the smallest hidden-dependence game that can support a strict
coordination-free improvement. Two anonymous agents search one of two targets
and split a unit prize when they select the true target. Their signals are
either one duplicated common-source draw or two conditionally independent
draws. The study compares a private-information anonymous symmetric Bayes–Nash
selection with a posterior-contingent shared-information anonymous symmetric
Nash selection, then audits all pure equilibria separately. The theorem target
is an exact `p=3/5` dependence interval, not a claim about every equilibrium or
arbitrary sharing environment.
