# Literature and novelty boundary

The team-information baseline is Radner (1962); Rosenthal (1973) owns the
congestion-game foundation. Public information and strategic congestion are
adjacent to Morris and Shin (2002) and modern Bayesian congestion work.
Correlated/common and independent information mixtures, social learning, and
strategic information design have extensive prior literatures, including
Bikhchandani, Hirshleifer, and Welch (1992), Kamenica and Gentzkow (2011), and
Bergemann and Morris (2016).

The eligible DD-022 contribution is narrow: an exact discovery objective under
a world-level hidden-dependence source mixture, a private-versus-public
comparison under one declared anonymous posterior-only selection, and a
certified strict-gain threshold. Public information helping agents, congestion
inducing dispersion, correlated signals, and anonymous mixing are not novelty
claims. Sharing reveals agreement or disagreement and updates beliefs about
dependence; it neither reveals the latent branch nor creates independence.
