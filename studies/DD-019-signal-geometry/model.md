# DD-019 model

Nature draws `theta` uniformly from four targets. Three conditionally
independent agents observe signals from a declared finite channel `W(s|theta)`.
For pooled observations `s^N`, `V_L` is the expected sum of the `L` largest
posterior target masses, for `L=1,2,3`. `V_1` is pooled consensus accuracy;
larger `L` assumes binding top-`L` planner authority and distinct actions.

One-person accuracy is expected posterior maximum. Direct private-portfolio
discovery uses independent uniform tie-breaking within each private Bayes
correspondence; for the registered symmetric channels it equals
`1-(1-q)^3`. This is not an unrestricted anonymous-policy or private-team
optimum. Recovery budget is the least registered `L` whose pooled value reaches
that named baseline, or null if none does.

Registered families are a half-accurate noisy point; a two-shortlist including
the target with probability `3/4`; a guaranteed two-shortlist; an explicit
uniformly selected excluded non-target; and point nominations with equally
likely low/high confidence and conditional accuracies `1/2` and `3/4`.
