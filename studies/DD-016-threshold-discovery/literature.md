## Evidence map

The project-specific object is a finite discovery architecture with one hidden
target, a posterior ranking over candidate actions, a threshold opening
technology, limited agents, strategic equal prize splitting, and both
discovery and portfolio objectives. None of threshold public goods, volunteer
dilemmas, congestion, clubs, coalition formation, strong equilibrium, or
stochastic selection is claimed as new.

The deterministic planner theorem follows from the declared atomic coverage
technology. Its useful contribution is a transparent specialization:
form as many size-tau teams as capacity allows on the highest posterior
candidates. It is not a theorem for production with increasing returns above
threshold, execution risk, robustness value, reusable evidence, or overlapping
coverage.

## Novelty and terminology risks

- Minimum winning coalitions and minimum contributor sets are established
  language; use minimum viable teams as explanatory project language only.
- A selected symmetric tied-mode rule is not a unique behavioral prediction.
- Nash equilibrium does not imply pairwise, strong, or coalition-proof
  stability.
- Necessary overlap below the threshold is not redundancy. Excess overlap is
  defined only relative to the registered atomic opening technology.
- Hard Boxes is an intuitive hook, not the academic name of the general
  object.
