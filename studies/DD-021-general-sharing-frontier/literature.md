# Literature search and theorem-name boundary

Search completed `2026-07-22` using publisher, society, DOI, JMLR, and arXiv
records. The DD-020 search on team decisions, jury rules, public information,
social learning, consensus, collective search, and list prediction remains the
immediate inherited boundary. DD-021 adds the finite Bayes-error ratio and
fixed-cardinality posterior-set questions. This review calibrates terminology;
it does not establish novelty.

## Search terms and result

Queries combined “Bayes error contraction,” “error contraction ratio,”
“additional independent observation,” “finite hypothesis testing,” “top-k
posterior mass,” “Bayes-optimal set-valued prediction,” “independent rescue,”
and “information sharing discovery.” Searches for the exact descriptive phrase
“Independent-Rescue Error-Contraction Criterion” returned no relevant
statistical or collective-search theorem. “Error contraction ratio” is already
generic language in numerical analysis and posterior-contraction work, so the
study uses the phrase only as a transparent local label and makes no naming or
priority claim.

## Primary and stable sources

- Blackwell, “Equivalent Comparisons of Experiments,” *Annals of Mathematical
  Statistics* 24(2), 1953, 265–272,
  [doi:10.1214/aoms/1177729032](https://doi.org/10.1214/aoms/1177729032).
  Blackwell owns the general comparison-of-experiments framework. DD-021 fixes
  a channel and changes an action protocol; it proposes no information order.
- Chernoff, “A Measure of Asymptotic Efficiency for Tests of a Hypothesis
  Based on the Sum of Observations,” *Annals of Mathematical Statistics*
  23(4), 1952, 493–507,
  [doi:10.1214/aoms/1177729330](https://doi.org/10.1214/aoms/1177729330).
  Chernoff information controls asymptotic testing error. DD-021's
  `rho_s=e_(s+1)/e_s` is a finite realized Bayes-error ratio and is not an
  exponent, asymptotic rate, divergence, or channel coefficient.
- Radner, “Team Decision Problems,” *Annals of Mathematical Statistics* 33(3),
  1962, 857–881,
  [doi:10.1214/aoms/1177704455](https://doi.org/10.1214/aoms/1177704455).
  DD-021 is a narrow common-payoff protocol comparison, not team optimality.
- Nitzan and Paroush, “Optimal Decision Rules in Uncertain Dichotomous Choice
  Situations,” *International Economic Review* 23(2), 1982, 289–297,
  [doi:10.2307/2526438](https://doi.org/10.2307/2526438). Committee-verdict
  accuracy is adjacent to `C_s`, but DD-021 compares that one-action gain with
  simultaneous independent search attempts.
- Mortier, Wydmuch, Dembczyński, Hüllermeier, and Waegeman, “Efficient
  Set-Valued Prediction in Multi-Class Classification,” 2019,
  [arXiv:1906.08129](https://arxiv.org/abs/1906.08129). Their decision-theoretic
  analysis makes explicit that, at fixed set size, the Bayes set consists of
  the largest posterior probabilities. DD-021 uses that elementary fixed-size
  fact for an observed target-search portfolio, without adopting their utility,
  learning, or approximation problem.
- Sadinle, Lei, and Wasserman, “Least Ambiguous Set-Valued Classifiers with
  Bounded Error Levels,” 2016,
  [arXiv:1609.00451](https://arxiv.org/abs/1609.00451). Coverage-versus-set-size
  calibration is adjacent to recovery budgets, but their estimand and learned
  classifier differ from a finite pooled-action capacity reaching a private
  portfolio.
- Mao, Mohri, and Zhong, “Top-k Classification and Cardinality-Aware
  Prediction,” 2024,
  [arXiv:2403.19625](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.19625). Top-`k` loss and
  cardinality-aware learning are not the same as exact posterior target mass
  under a fixed atomic action technology.

## Eligible contribution and novelty boundary

The eligible contribution is the exact accounting criterion inside the
declared protocol, its proof-audited centralized recovery implication, and an
independently reproduced bounded channel classification. No claim is made that
the algebraic identity, Bayes MAP rule, top-`L` rule, error-ratio terminology,
or broad aggregation/diversity tradeoff is new. Any paper-level novelty claim
requires the later editorial gate and a deeper referee-oriented review.
