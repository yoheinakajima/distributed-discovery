# Independent internal proof audit

Audit date: `2026-07-22`. This audit restarts from the frozen model and does
not use the bounded registry as proof.

## Audit result

The recovered DD-020 identity, adjacent error form, error-contraction
equivalences, and full-capacity pooled-planner dominance are internally
consistent under the declared assumptions. No theorem extends to dependent
sources, nonconstant direct accuracy, strategic action, non-atomic technology,
or decentralized implementation.

## Frontier obligations

1. **Target conditioning.** Failure factorization occurs only after fixing the
   target; conditional source and tie independence are explicit.
2. **Scalar reduction.** Target symmetry and constant direct accuracy justify
   replacing target-specific failures by `C_s` and `q`.
3. **Adjacent algebra.** Expanding `G_(s+1)-G_s` yields the stated factor and
   no reversed sign; substituting `e_s=1-C_s` gives
   `(1-q)e_s-e_(s+1)`.
4. **Sign equivalence.** The residual multiplier is positive unless `q=1`.
   The perfect-direct boundary is separately neutral because the signal/direct
   rule identifies the target and pooled MAP error is zero.
5. **Zero error.** When `e_s=0`, an additional observation cannot increase
   Bayes error because the old MAP decision remains available while the new MAP
   is Bayes-optimal. The ratio is correctly left undefined.
6. **Terminology.** `rho_s` is a finite Bayes-error ratio, not an asymptotic
   exponent, contraction theorem, divergence, or information coefficient.

## Full-capacity obligations

1. **Duplicates.** The realized direct-action union has cardinality no larger
   than `N`; duplicate actions shrink it and never invalidate the comparison.
2. **Ties.** The proof fixes private tie outcomes and dominates each realized
   union. A posterior tie at the top-`L` boundary preserves total mass.
3. **`N>M`.** Choosing all `M` atomic targets gives posterior mass one and
   makes the inequality immediate.
4. **Authority.** `V_L` assumes binding centralized selection of distinct
   target actions after observing all signals. The result is not labeled a
   decentralized implementation.
5. **Random private actions.** The planner does not need private tie seeds;
   pointwise domination before averaging is sufficient.
6. **Endpoint.** `V_1` equals pooled MAP accuracy `C_N`, so the `L*=1`
   equivalence uses exactly the same baseline and tie convention.

## Tightness and failure boundary

Full-capacity dominance can be equality or strict; the bounded registry will
record both without promoting a general strictness statement. Reducing the
capacity below the realized direct-action union size can fail, which is why
the proof does not claim `V_L>=P_N` for arbitrary `L`. Changing to threshold,
coverage, repeated-value, or unreliable actions requires a new theorem.

## Computational role

Exact enumeration, method agreement, corruption rejection, and hand cases may
detect implementation or transcription errors. They are regression evidence,
not the reason either analytic theorem is true.
