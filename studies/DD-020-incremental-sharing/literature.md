# Literature search and novelty boundary

Search completed `2026-07-22` against publisher pages and stable DOI records.
The purpose is boundary setting, not a claim that this list is exhaustive.

## Closest foundations

- Blackwell, “Equivalent Comparisons of Experiments,” *Annals of Mathematical
  Statistics* 24(2), 1953, 265–272,
  [doi:10.1214/aoms/1177729032](https://doi.org/10.1214/aoms/1177729032).
  Blackwell orders experiments by their value across decision problems.
  DD-020 instead fixes one experiment and compares action protocols with a
  group-level one-hit objective; it does not claim a new experiment order.
- Radner, “Team Decision Problems,” *Annals of Mathematical Statistics* 33(3),
  1962, 857–881,
  [doi:10.1214/aoms/1177704455](https://doi.org/10.1214/aoms/1177704455).
  This is foundational team decision theory. DD-020 is a deliberately narrow
  common-payoff finite team protocol, not a general team-optimality result.
- Nitzan and Paroush, “Optimal Decision Rules in Uncertain Dichotomous Choice
  Situations,” *International Economic Review* 23(2), 1982, 289–297,
  [doi:10.2307/2526438](https://doi.org/10.2307/2526438). Their binary collective
  decision rules are adjacent to DD-020's binary theorem, but DD-020 evaluates
  discovery by a union of actions rather than a single committee verdict.
- Austen-Smith and Banks, “Information Aggregation, Rationality, and the
  Condorcet Jury Theorem,” *American Political Science Review* 90(1), 1996,
  34–45, [doi:10.2307/2082796](https://doi.org/10.2307/2082796). This cautions
  that informational aggregation and individual behavior cannot be separated
  automatically. DD-020 assumes a nonstrategic common-payoff protocol and does
  not inherit a Condorcet-style welfare or equilibrium conclusion.

## Public information, consensus, and social learning

- DeGroot, “Reaching a Consensus,” *Journal of the American Statistical
  Association* 69(345), 1974, 118–121,
  [doi:10.1080/01621459.1974.10480137](https://doi.org/10.1080/01621459.1974.10480137).
  Repeated opinion pooling and convergence are not the simultaneous one-block
  protocol studied here.
- Banerjee, “A Simple Model of Herd Behavior,” *Quarterly Journal of Economics*
  107(3), 1992, 797–817,
  [doi:10.2307/2118364](https://doi.org/10.2307/2118364), and Bikhchandani,
  Hirshleifer, and Welch, “A Theory of Fads, Fashion, Custom, and Cultural
  Change as Informational Cascades,” *Journal of Political Economy* 100(5),
  1992, 992–1026, [doi:10.1086/261849](https://doi.org/10.1086/261849).
  Both study sequential observation and endogenous conformity. DD-020 has no
  sequence, strategic inference from actions, or cascade claim.
- Morris and Shin, “Social Value of Public Information,” *American Economic
  Review* 92(5), 2002, 1521–1534,
  [doi:10.1257/000282802762024610](https://doi.org/10.1257/000282802762024610).
  Their public-signal result turns on coordination motives. DD-020's loss of
  independent rescue is instead mechanical under a common discovery event.
- Acemoglu, Dahleh, Lobel, and Ozdaglar, “Bayesian Learning in Social
  Networks,” *Review of Economic Studies* 78(4), 2011, 1201–1236,
  [doi:10.1093/restud/rdr004](https://doi.org/10.1093/restud/rdr004). DD-020
  does not study asymptotic learning, observational networks, or equilibrium.

## Collective search, diversity, and action lists

- Hong and Page, “Groups of Diverse Problem Solvers Can Outperform Groups of
  High-Ability Problem Solvers,” *PNAS* 101(46), 2004, 16385–16389,
  [doi:10.1073/pnas.0403723101](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.0403723101).
  Their functional diversity/search framework motivates the value of distinct
  attempts, but its search landscape and group-composition theorem do not imply
  DD-020's finite Bayesian identity.
- Lazer and Friedman, “The Network Structure of Exploration and Exploitation,”
  *Administrative Science Quarterly* 52(4), 2007, 667–694,
  [doi:10.2189/asqu.52.4.667](https://doi.org/10.2189/asqu.52.4.667). Their
  dynamic simulation shows a communication/diversity tradeoff over time;
  DD-020 is exact, one-shot, and non-networked.
- Set-valued and list prediction is the closest statistical analogy to keeping
  several candidate actions. Chzhen, Denis, Hebiri, and Lorieul,
  “Set-valued classification—overview via a unified framework,” 2021,
  [arXiv:2102.12318](https://arxiv.org/abs/2102.12318), gives a stable survey
  and decision-theoretic formulation. DD-020 does not estimate a classifier or
  optimize list size; DD-019 supplies the repository's exact action-budget
  comparison and DD-020 changes block size at a fixed one-action pool.

## Ownership and novelty statement

The literature already owns broad claims that aggregation can improve a
single verdict, that communication can suppress diversity or private
information, and that independent or diverse search can help a collective.
This study therefore makes **no first-discovery, field-novelty, majority-rule,
herding, consensus, or general information-sharing claim**. Its eligible
contribution is limited to the registered aggregation–rescue identity, the
proof for the declared symmetric noisy-point protocol, the bounded exact
census, and the DD-019 channel comparison. Any broader relation is context.
