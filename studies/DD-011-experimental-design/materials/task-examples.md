# Draft task examples

If two team members both choose target A and A is correct, discovery succeeds but
there is one distinct action. If they choose A and B and either is correct,
discovery succeeds with two distinct actions. A shared source may give both members
the same clue; independent sources are drawn separately conditional on the target.

Examples are pedagogical and do not reveal future realizations or hidden signals.

In a selective-attention example, four roles with private clues may receive one
public clue. Broadcast lets all four condition on it; one-reader access lets only
one role do so; a nonbinding recommendation leaves use voluntary; and a license
makes one-reader conditioning the registered feasible action. A conditional role
may use the public clue on agreement and a registered private or third-option
response on disagreement. These examples distinguish access from recommendation.
