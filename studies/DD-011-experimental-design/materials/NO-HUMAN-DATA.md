# Ethics and data boundary

> **No participants were recruited. No human data were collected. No experiment
> was conducted. Separate ethics and institutional review are required before
> deployment.**

The current repository phase prohibits real participant records, recruitment,
intervention, surveys, and live assignment. Any future deployment requires an
institution-specific ethics review, a named data controller, security review,
adverse-event procedure, consent approval, and a separately authorized protocol.

Proposed minimization: collect only a random study ID, assigned condition,
task responses, timing rounded to seconds, comprehension results, and payment
accounting kept in a separate access-controlled system. Do not collect names,
email, employer, investment history, health data, precise location, contacts, or
free-form sensitive text. Proposed de-identification removes the assignment key;
the proposed retention window is twelve months after final analysis, subject to
institutional policy.

Withdrawal draft: a participant may stop without penalty beyond the disclosed
minimum-payment rule and request deletion before the de-identification cutoff.
Adverse-event draft: pause the session, preserve no unnecessary narrative detail,
notify the responsible investigator and ethics body, and do not resume until cleared.
