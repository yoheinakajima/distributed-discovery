# DD-002 research brief — Information Design for Discovery

## Motivating question

Can a Blackwell-more-informative public disclosure reduce equilibrium discovery because strategically autonomous searchers concentrate on the same high-posterior action? The question is about implemented equilibrium discovery, not the information frontier, which remains weakly monotone under emulation.

## Minimum viable model

- One target \(\theta\in\{0,1,2\}\) with uniform prior.
- A designer observes finite pooled evidence \(S\) generated by a declared likelihood matrix.
- The designer commits ex ante to a public disclosure kernel \(\sigma(y\mid s)\) with finite message set \(Y\).
- Two searchers observe the same message, form the induced posterior, and simultaneously choose one location each.
- Rewards use the canonical equal-split prize; social value is union discovery.
- An equilibrium-selection rule is part of the model. Begin with anonymous symmetric equilibrium and separately record all pure equilibria.

The comparison fixes the action game and changes only disclosure. Full pooling, full revelation, and every deterministic partition of the evidence states are feasible first benchmarks. Randomized disclosure is deferred until deterministic partitions are audited.

## Relationship to the canonical benchmark

The canonical paper takes pooled reports as given and compares consensus, market, private, and planner protocols. DD-002 endogenizes the public information available to the strategic market while holding its reward/action technology fixed. It sits between Blackwell comparison and the canonical action-concentration mechanism; it does not reinterpret the canonical copying crossover.

## Main quantities

- \(W(\sigma)\): discovery at the declared selected equilibrium.
- \(V_N(I_\sigma)\): pooled planner frontier under the same disclosed message.
- Disclosure protocol loss \(V_N(I_\sigma)-W(\sigma)\).
- Expected distinct actions and action Herfindahl concentration.
- Information order or mutual-information summaries, reported separately from welfare.
- Best/worst equilibrium discovery when selection is not unique.

## Adjacent literature

Blackwell (1953) supplies experiment comparison; Kamenica and Gentzkow (2011) supply Bayesian persuasion; Acemoglu et al. (2018) supply a direct informational-Braess precedent. Canonical covering-game and equal-split results define the action stage. The study must not claim that harmful information is novel.

## Likely methods

Exact rational posterior enumeration; deterministic partition enumeration; water-filling equilibrium evaluation; equilibrium correspondence checks; small linear programs for randomized disclosure only after an ADR and certificate path; symbolic comparison for the smallest counterexample.

## Falsifiable questions

1. Does a pair of Blackwell-comparable deterministic disclosures exist with strictly lower selected-equilibrium discovery under the more informative one?
2. If so, is the reversal robust across every equilibrium, or selection-specific?
3. Does planner discovery remain monotone while market discovery falls in the same pair?
4. Can a less informative disclosure reduce action concentration without sacrificing enough ranking quality to offset the gain?

Negative results on the complete minimum model are publishable constraints, not evidence for larger games.

## Dependencies and risks

Depends on DD-000 frontier notation and the pinned market evaluator; DD-001 clarifies information-versus-assignment comparisons. Major risks are equilibrium multiplicity, accidental changes to rewards while changing disclosure, confusing Blackwell order with scalar informativeness, and overfitting a numerical counterexample.

## First executable experiment

Enumerate a rational \(M=3,N=2\) likelihood fixture with at most four evidence states. List every deterministic evidence partition, compute induced posteriors and all pure/anonymous symmetric equilibria exactly, and output ordered disclosure pairs with discovery and concentration. Precompute the number of partitions and cap the fixture search before execution.

## Completion criteria

- Disclosure and equilibrium selection are fully specified.
- Every reported reversal has an exact witness and independent evaluator.
- Blackwell comparability is certified, not inferred from entropy.
- Planner and equilibrium effects are reported separately.
- A null exhaustive result is retained with its search boundary.
