# Frozen aligned model

There are three uniform targets and two agents. Evidence is either one common
`2/3`-accurate signal or two conditionally independent signals of the same
accuracy; independent evidence costs `1/8` per agent. Disclosure is private or
pooled. Allocation is direct, consensus, one-answer market, two-action planner,
perfect-elimination sequential, or the DD-006 differentiated recommendation.
Timing is simultaneous except for the sequential protocol. Rewards are chosen
from equal split, pooling, sole rescue, marginal coverage, DD-006A, and DD-006B
only where their inputs and signal law are coherent.

The planner benchmark chooses the registered two-label differentiated portfolio
from the same signals. Social net value subtracts information cost and external
transfer budget. These are synthetic accounting conventions, not welfare
weights for an institution.
