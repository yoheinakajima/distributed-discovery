# Exact two-searcher thresholds

The informative hybrid witnesses are generated by one role family. One searcher covers a fixed territory; the other follows all signals except the territorial signal, which is rerouted to a second target. Its value is \(H_M(p)=[1+(M-2)p]/(M-1)\). Compared with \(D_M(p)=2p-p^2\) and \(T_M(p)=2/M\),

\[
H_M-D_M=\frac{(1-p)(1-(M-1)p)}{M-1},\qquad
H_M-T_M=\frac{(M-2)(Mp-1)}{M(M-1)}.
\]

Thus the restricted phase sequence is territorial, hybrid, direct, with thresholds \(1/M\) and \(1/(M-1)\). Exact polynomial enumeration proves the same unrestricted informative envelope for \(M=3,4,5\). The all-\(M\) informative extension remains conjectural. The restriction cannot be dropped: at \(p=0\), no-fixed-point signal policies beat all three families for \(M=3,4\).
