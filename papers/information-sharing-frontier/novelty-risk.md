# Novelty-risk note

Status: editorial risk assessment, not evidence and not a novelty opinion.

## High-risk wording to avoid

- “Information sharing is beneficial” or “information sharing is harmful.”
- “The first theory” or “a new field/order of information.”
- “A common posterior implements the planner.”
- “One-person accuracy characterizes a channel.”
- “The positive interval holds in every equilibrium.”
- “The 177-scenario registry proves arbitrary-channel monotonicity.”
- “Sharing reveals whether signals came from the common source.”

## Defensible contribution boundary

The manuscript may present a unified theorem family for a finite discovery
objective: scalar accuracy can fail to determine an action-budget profile;
incremental sharing trades aggregation against independent rescue; the sign is
exactly governed by residual-error contraction; centralized action capacity can
recover the named private baseline; and one anonymous posterior-only selected
equilibrium admits a certified positive interval while the full correspondence
destroys an every-equilibrium conclusion.

Each component is adjacent to established work. The editorial value lies in
their exact composition and in keeping information, action budget, authority,
reward, and equilibrium selection visible at once. The paper makes no claim of
priority, universal novelty, journal fit, external validity, or empirical
effect.
