# Information Design for Discovery — bounded paper outline

1. **Question and boundary.** Public evidence can change equilibrium allocation without reducing the planner information frontier; connect to Blackwell order, persuasion, and informational Braess effects without a novelty claim.
2. **Finite model.** Uniform three-target prior, declared four-state likelihood, deterministic public partitions, two equal-split searchers, union discovery, full pure correspondence, and anonymous symmetric selection.
3. **Complete enumeration.** Prove the 15-partition count, store 37 posterior games and 45 strict refinement pairs, and explain deterministic garbling witnesses.
4. **Selection-dependent reversal.** Present the pooling and two-block posteriors, exact equilibria, \(5/9\) versus \(171/308\), and the opposing \(2/3\) versus \(3/4\) pure/planner comparison.
5. **Bounded census.** Report one selected reversal, eight worst-pure reversals, zero best-pure reversals, and planner monotonicity throughout.
6. **Limits and next work.** The likelihood is one finite fixture; randomized disclosure and asymmetric mixed equilibria are deferred.
