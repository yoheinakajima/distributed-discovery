We use \emph{distributed discovery} as a working label for the design and analysis of systems that convert dispersed evidence into a finite portfolio of search actions. A discovery architecture has two distinct components. Its information architecture determines what agents can infer, while its action protocol determines how available capacity is distributed across candidates. The shared discovery paradox arises when the information architecture improves the ranking but the action protocol compresses the portfolio. The label organizes the extensions below; it is not a claim that the phrase or the surrounding literatures are new.
