# Claim prominence policy

Prominence answers an editorial question: which role should a validated claim
play in a paper, synthesis, review, or reading path? It is not evidence status.
The eight allowed categories are fixed in `claim-prominence.yml`.

Every live claim must appear exactly once. Prominence may influence ordering,
badges, public eligibility, synthesis role, or review priority, but it may not
rewrite, conceal, or infer claim type, status, scope, source, run, proof, or
verification. A “spine/title theorem” label means title-level editorial use
within the claim's existing scope; it does not promote the claim's evidence.

The mapping is deliberately auditable and reversible. Scientific corrections
must be made through the claim ledger and its promotion rules, never through
this file.
