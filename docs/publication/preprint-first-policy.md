# Preprint-first publication policy

Status: owner-adopted. The active publication track is preprint-first; the
journal track is deferred behind `journal-track-policy.md`. Journals are not
abandoned, and no submission is authorized by this policy.

A preprint freeze is a paper-specific gate. It requires a self-contained
central argument, competent primary-source related work, honest working-paper
status, conditional rather than unsupported novelty language, explicit limits
and counterboundaries, deterministic PDF and source bundle, licensing review,
and evidence-layer provenance. The scholarly narrative should remove internal
issue/PR/gate and repository-acceptance language while preserving claim, run,
checksum, source, and proof provenance in footnotes, appendices, or companion
metadata.

The site and repository are a living compendium for discoverability, evidence
routing, exact artifacts, claims, runs, Labs, and relationships. They are not
an ever-growing referee appendix and cannot be the sole load-bearing scholarly
source. Internal queue order, venue options, and estimated spacing are not
public promises.

Every freeze rechecks current target-service category, moderation,
endorsement, licensing, source-package, and cross-list rules. Dated policy
observations do not become permanent scientific metadata.
