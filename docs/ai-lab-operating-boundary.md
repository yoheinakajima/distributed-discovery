# AI-powered lab operating boundary

Distributed Discovery is operated as an AI-powered research lab under human
PI ownership and final gate authority. This boundary governs internal process;
it does not establish scientific quality, autonomy, external validation, or
peer review.

## Authorized internal roles

The lab may assign AI systems to ten separated roles:

1. produce a derivation or implementation;
2. reproduce it from context-isolated inputs;
3. verify it by a materially distinct method;
4. attack proofs, scope, assumptions, and counterexamples;
5. test for reduction to established classical results;
6. inspect primary-source literature and terminology collisions;
7. corrupt inputs, certificates, manifests, schemas, and outputs;
8. audit editorial ownership, qualifiers, and claim boundaries;
9. integrate accepted artifacts without changing their evidence status; and
10. run final acceptance against predeclared invariants.

Role separation is an architecture requirement, not evidence that separate
people, organizations, providers, or model families participated. Context
isolation records exactly what a role received. Distinct-method review records
the independent mathematical or computational route, not merely a fresh
prompt. Any unresolved disagreement is preserved.

## Human PI authority

The PI owns program direction and makes final decisions on scope, scientific
promotion, publication, cost, data access, deployment, and external action.
AI systems may prepare evidence and recommendations within an authorized
scope; they do not widen that scope or exercise reserved gates.

## Outside the lab

Human-engagement operations are outside the lab until a new explicit owner
decision authorizes them. The active program therefore has no external-reader
pipeline, reviewer packet, seminar or reading-group plan, coauthor pipeline,
reviewer-selection process, outreach or endorsement workflow, feedback
tracker, human holdout custodian, or adoption requirement. No person is
designated as a reviewer, coauthor candidate, custodian, or interlocutor.

## Language boundary

Internal AI audits must not be described as peer review, external review,
independent scholarly validation, community acceptance, or outside
reproduction. A context-isolated reproduction remains internal. A cross-model
adversarial audit, if separately authorized later, remains an internal
robustness check. Level 5 maturity is never “external validation.”

## Future change

Human engagement, provider calls, cross-model evaluation, sealed agent-system
evaluation, real data, or production deployment each requires a new explicit
owner decision and its own evidence, cost, safety, and reproducibility gate.
