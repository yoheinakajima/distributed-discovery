# Disciplinary home

Operations research and management science is the default disciplinary and
rhetorical home for Distributed Discovery. The program's central object is a
discovery architecture: a rule system that converts dispersed evidence into a
portfolio of actions under budgets, incentives, authority, reliability, and
coordination constraints.

Economic theory and algorithmic game theory are secondary mathematical
adjacencies. They supply information comparison, team decision, information
design, equilibrium, implementation, congestion, covering, welfare-sharing,
and price-of-anarchy foundations. Multi-agent AI is the audience for
DiscoveryBench Agents, whose future evidence will be system-, model-,
provider-, prompt-, topology-, and version-specific.

Practitioner language is a translation layer only. It is not evidence of
deployment, adoption, usefulness, or empirical validation. Methods discipline
improves auditability but does not prove scientific quality.

The internal possibility that this program extends an open search-theory
tradition is gated and prohibited on public surfaces. Before any succession
framing becomes public, a separate primary-source literature and coinage gate
must identify the claimed open panel, compare search theory, team decision,
parallel search, coverage, organization design, information design, and
multi-agent allocation, document terminology collisions, and produce a
surviving non-overlap statement. No venue targeting, seminar plan, reviewer
plan, or outreach workflow is authorized.
