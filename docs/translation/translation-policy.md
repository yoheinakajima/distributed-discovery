# Community translation policy

Scientific statements have one canonical object and may have several
defensible community formulations. Translation helps readers locate the object
without changing its scope, status, ownership, or evidence.

The authoritative concordance is separate from `claims/claims.yml`. It
distinguishes exact equivalents, standard-literature neighbors, approximate
restatements, analogies, and practitioner formulations. Every formulation
identifies the canonical object, supporting claims, studies and papers,
literature adjacency, a mandatory qualifier, prohibited phrasing, and approved
public surfaces.

Translation cannot create synonyms for discoverability, imply adoption, turn
an analogy into an equivalence, or transfer primary editorial ownership.
Unsupported synonym proliferation and keyword spam are prohibited. Public
rendering uses the registry rather than duplicating variants in page source.

AI search-rank monitoring, AI-referrer tracking, retrieval-query monitoring,
citation-rank monitoring, and semantic-visibility dashboards are out of
program scope. The only adjacent monitoring permitted is future contamination
detection for DiscoveryBench Agents, including exact or near-verbatim public
benchmark values, wording, task IDs, or solution patterns in evaluated output.
