# Paper recomposition policy

Paper boundaries may change while scientific claims, studies, proofs,
certificates, and immutable runs remain durable. Recomposition works from the
claim ledger and theorem architecture rather than copying manuscripts in
chronological order.

A proposed merge, split, absorption, demotion, retirement, or supersession
must:

1. define one central question;
2. select claims from the ledger;
3. assign primary, supporting, background, and counterboundary roles;
4. assign unique primary editorial ownership;
5. identify every predecessor paper;
6. write from theorem architecture rather than chronological copying;
7. audit scientific equivalence and scope;
8. audit literature adjacency and mandatory counterboundaries;
9. build and accept the replacement;
10. only then change predecessor lifecycle; and
11. preserve stable routes, PDFs, hashes, replacement links, and citation
   instructions.

Retirement without replacement is permitted only when no scientific claim
loses an owner or route and the historical artifact retains a citation.
Withdrawal for error follows the separate scientific-correction rule.
Recomposition never changes claim status.

This milestone assesses options only. It performs no merge, split, absorption,
demotion, retirement, archive, withdrawal, or supersession.
