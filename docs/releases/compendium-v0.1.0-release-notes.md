# Distributed Discovery Research Compendium v0.1.0

> Offline candidate notes. Version `0.1.0` has not been tagged or published,
> and no DOI exists.

Distributed Discovery studies how organizations and multi-agent systems turn
dispersed evidence into portfolios of search actions. This compendium is a
software and research-infrastructure archive designed to bind source,
scientific ledgers, immutable run records, reproducibility methods, public
benchmark infrastructure, and exact paper artifacts to one future revision.

## Inventory and status

The candidate records 110 claims through `DD-C-0110`, 26 registered studies
through `DD-022`, 51 immutable run manifests, and 48 passing immutable runs.
It includes seven exact local paper PDFs totaling 119 pages together with
declared reproduction source. Their lifecycle remains working paper, research
note, or synthesis note. No local paper is represented as submitted, accepted,
peer reviewed, journal published, or externally validated.

TreasureBench is the formal benchmark name and Treasure Hunt is its playable
companion. Public provider-calibration infrastructure is included, but there
is no sealed-pilot or private benchmark evidence and this release tooling made
no provider call.

## Reproduction and evidence

Use the immutable future source revision, release evidence manifest, and
`SHA256SUMS` together. Scientific statements should cite the compendium version
and, where load-bearing, the claim ID, immutable run ID, and artifact checksum.
The repository's reproducibility instructions describe pinned inputs,
validation, seeds, outputs, and provenance.

The Shared Discovery Paradox remains the separate canonical upstream result at
the pinned upstream commit. This compendium includes the lock, attribution, and
additive integration metadata; it does not duplicate the upstream paper.

## Limitations

The archive does not imply peer review, legal clearance, DOI registration,
Zenodo activation, package publication, arXiv submission, journal submission,
or new scientific evidence. External identifiers remain null until observed
from a completed authorized publication workflow.
