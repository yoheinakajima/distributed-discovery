# Glossary — DD-000

Evidence labels are noted in parentheses: **definition**, **identity**, **upstream**, or **provisional**.

- **Action concentration (provisional):** concentration of action mass across candidates under a stated index and unit.
- **Action protocol (definition):** stochastic mapping from available information histories to feasible joint actions.
- **Action redundancy (definition family):** collision or overlap in a portfolio under a declared action/coverage functional.
- **Average action quality (upstream):** mean standalone probability that an action hits the atomic target.
- **Communication budget (provisional):** stated constraint on message bits, rounds, topology, or transcripts.
- **Coverage technology (definition):** map from world and action portfolio/sequence to discovered outcomes or value.
- **Discovery architecture (definition):** information architecture, action protocol, and reward rule \((I,\Pi,R)\).
- **Discovery budget (definition):** feasible constraint on actions/resources; may be cardinality, cost, time, or role capacity.
- **Discovery price of anarchy (definition family):** planner/equilibrium discovery ratio for a stated game and equilibrium class.
- **Distributed Discovery (working definition):** study of converting dispersed evidence into portfolios of search actions; phrase is not claimed unique.
- **Effective channels (upstream/model-specific; provisional generally):** distinct generative evidence draws in the canonical copying model; no general functional yet.
- **Information architecture (definition):** allocation of evidence, messages, and histories across decision makers and times.
- **Information frontier (definition):** supremum discovery over a declared feasible protocol class for fixed information and other primitives.
- **Information–Action Decomposition (identity):** \(G=V-L\) after protocol loss is defined as \(V-G\).
- **Private-team frontier (provisional DD-001):** best discovery under ex-ante coordinated local-signal policies without action-time communication.
- **Protocol loss (definition):** frontier minus realized protocol discovery for the same declared architecture and budget.
- **Recovery budget (definition):** smallest budget whose frontier reaches a stated benchmark value.
- **Redundant hits (identity):** \(\mathbb E[(K-1)^+]\) in the atomic one-target model.
- **Reward/credit rule (definition):** mapping from actions/outcomes to agent utility or credit, distinct from the social objective.
- **Shared Discovery Paradox (upstream):** shared information raises average action quality while reducing union discovery under the compared protocol.
- **Source concentration (provisional):** concentration of evidence provenance over declared latent/observed source units.
- **TreasureBench (editorial name):** the formal public DD-010 suite; canonical subtitle: “TreasureBench: a benchmark for collective search under shared and private evidence.” DiscoveryBench remains its historical/internal compatibility alias.
- **Treasure Hunt (presentation label):** the playable companion to TreasureBench, not a benchmark, Lab, schema prefix, metric, claim, run, or theorem name.
