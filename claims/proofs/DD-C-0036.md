# DD-C-0036 — exact canonical private-team interval

## Claim

In the canonical deterministic zero-communication private-team model,

```text
325089/390625
  <= T_8(16,1/5)
  <= 860391662035297/1001129150390625.
```

The exact interval width is
`27224111644672/1001129150390625`, approximately
`0.0271934061994395`.

## Lower inequality

Direct clue-following is a feasible deterministic private-team profile. Its
conditional failures are independent, each with probability `4/5`, so its
discovery value is

```text
1-(4/5)^8 = (5^8-4^8)/5^8 = 325089/390625.
```

Maximizing over the full feasible private-team class cannot yield less.

## Upper inequality

Fix any deterministic private-team role profile. After observing the complete
private-report vector, the pooled planner can apply each role policy to its report
and emit exactly the same eight-action vector. Thus every private-team outcome
distribution is feasible for the pooled planner under the matched physical budget
and discovery objective.

Conditional on the pooled reports, discovery from an eight-action portfolio is
the posterior mass of the distinct labels it covers. Replacing duplicates by an
uncovered label cannot reduce this mass, and the eight largest posterior masses
maximize it. Therefore the pooled top-eight frontier weakly dominates the value
of every emulated private-team profile.

The exact pooled endpoint is independently computed in claim DD-C-0035. Its
labeled-count and histogram/orbit enumerations both give

```text
860391662035297/1001129150390625.
```

Taking the maximum over private-team profiles proves the upper inequality.

## Scope boundary

The proof requires pooled observation of all private reports, compliance with the
emulated actions, the same eight-action physical budget, and the same union-
discovery objective. It does not construct a private-team profile attaining the
upper endpoint. Neither endpoint is asserted to equal the unresolved private-team
optimum, and the interval is not called tight.
