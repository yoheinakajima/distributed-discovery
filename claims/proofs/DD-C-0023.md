# DD-C-0023 — lossless and feasible policy signatures

## Claim

In the finite DD-001 model, the signature $\sigma(f)=((c_t,d_t))_t$ determines a policy's contribution to every fixed-profile discovery value. The local count conditions plus the residual singleton Hall bounds are necessary and sufficient for signature realizability.

## Objective proof

Let $q=(1-p)/(M-1)$. Conditional on target $t$, the signal equals $t$ with probability $p$, while each other signal has probability $q$. Of the $c_t$ signals mapped to action $t$, the correct signal is included exactly when $d_t=1$. Thus

\[
s(t)=p d_t+q(c_t-d_t)=q c_t+(p-q)d_t.
\]

Conditional signal independence gives the profile value

\[
G=\frac1M\sum_t\left[1-\prod_i(1-s_i(t))\right],
\]

which contains each policy only through its signature.

## Feasibility proof

The local conditions are $c_t\in\mathbb Z_{\ge0}$, $d_t\in\{0,1\}$, $d_t\le c_t$, and $\sum_t c_t=M$. Remove every row with $d_t=1$ after assigning it to its diagonal column. There are $n_0$ remaining rows and residual column capacities $r_t=c_t-d_t$, whose total is $n_0$.

A remaining row $x$ may use every column except $x$. Duplicate column $t$ into $r_t$ slots. For singleton row set $\{x\}$, Hall's condition is

\[
n_0-r_x\ge1,
\]

or $r_x\le n_0-1$. For any row subset with at least two elements, every column slot is adjacent to at least one row in the subset: if the slot's column equals one selected row, a different selected row is adjacent to it. Its neighborhood is therefore all $n_0$ slots, which is at least the subset size. Hall's condition holds for every subset, so a perfect residual matching exists.

Conversely, any policy supplies such a matching. A nonfixed column cannot receive its own nonfixed row, so its residual count is at most (n_0-1); a fixed column can receive at most all (n_0) remaining rows. This proves necessity and sufficiency. The matching plus the removed diagonal assignments construct a realizing policy.

## Internal consistency review

The proof covers $n_0=0$ vacuously. When $n_0=1$, the sole nonfixed column must have residual zero and the row must use a fixed column, exactly as the bound requires. The reference implementation constructs the duplicated-slot matching; the reduced checker decides only the displayed conditions, so their exhaustive agreement is not circular.
