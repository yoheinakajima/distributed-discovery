# DD-C-0030 — selection-dependent disclosure reversal

Full pooling induces posterior \((1/3,1/3,1/3)\). Its anonymous symmetric equilibrium uses \((1/3,1/3,1/3)\), giving discovery \(5/9\). Its six labeled pure equilibria choose distinct locations and each gives discovery \(2/3\).

The finer disclosure has blocks \(\{0,1\}\) and \(\{2,3\}\), with probabilities \(5/8\) and \(3/8\). Mapping both fine messages to the pooled message is an explicit deterministic garbling, so the fine disclosure is Blackwell-more-informative.

The first fine message induces posterior \((1/3,2/5,4/15)\). Its anonymous symmetric equilibrium is \((4/11,7/11,0)\), with discovery \(6/11\); its two labeled pure equilibria choose locations 0 and 1 and give \(11/15\).

The second fine message induces posterior \((1/3,2/9,4/9)\). Its anonymous symmetric equilibrium is \((2/7,0,5/7)\), with discovery \(4/7\); its two labeled pure equilibria choose locations 0 and 2 and give \(7/9\).

Thus selected discovery under the refinement is

\[
\frac58\frac6{11}+\frac38\frac47=\frac{171}{308}
=\frac59-\frac1{2772},
\]

while every global pure-equilibrium selection gives

\[
\frac58\frac{11}{15}+\frac38\frac79=\frac34>\frac23.
\]

The two-action planner also rises from \(2/3\) to \(3/4\). The result is therefore a selection-dependent equilibrium reversal, not a decrease in the information frontier or an all-equilibrium reversal.
