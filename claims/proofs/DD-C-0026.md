# DD-C-0026 — exact three-family threshold

For two direct clue-followers, \(D_M(p)=1-(1-p)^2=2p-p^2\). Two distinct constant territories discover exactly two of \(M\) uniformly likely targets, so \(T_M(p)=2/M\).

For the one-reroute hybrid, one searcher always covers target 0. The other maps signal 0 to target 1 and otherwise follows the signal. Target 1 is found with probability \(p+q\), every target from 2 through \(M-1\) with probability \(p\), and target 0 surely, where \(q=(1-p)/(M-1)\). Therefore

\[
H_M(p)=\frac{1+(M-1)p+q}{M}
=\frac{1+(M-2)p}{M-1}.
\]

The exact differences factor as

\[
H_M(p)-D_M(p)=\frac{(1-p)(1-(M-1)p)}{M-1},
\]

and

\[
H_M(p)-T_M(p)=\frac{(M-2)(Mp-1)}{M(M-1)}.
\]

For \(M\ge3\), their signs give the stated territorial, hybrid, and direct regions and the ties at \(p=1/M\) and \(p=1/(M-1)\). At \(p=1\), direct and hybrid tie. This proves only the comparison of the three declared families.
