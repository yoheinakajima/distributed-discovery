# DD-C-0043 — complete pairwise moments do not determine discovery

Consider two sources with accuracies `1/2` and `2/3`. In the first colored
network the `1/2` source reaches only searcher four and the `2/3` source reaches
all four searchers:

```text
1/2: 0001
2/3: 1111
```

In the second network those source degrees are reversed:

```text
1/2: 1111
2/3: 0001
```

The networks are not color-preserving isomorphic because the degree attached to
each accuracy differs. Direct exact enumeration of the three targets, nine source
signal pairs, plurality-set reports, and independent-uniform action draws gives
the same four first-moment vectors and all six `3 x 3` pairwise action-moment
matrices. Their canonical pair-agreement signature is
`(17/24,17/24,17/24,1,1,1)`.

Private union discovery nevertheless equals `3/4` in the first network and `2/3`
in the second, a difference of `1/12`. The primary implementation and the separate
verifier reconstruct the complete 66-entry moment signature and discovery values
from the raw colored adjacencies. Replacing the stored discovery difference with
zero is rejected. Thus complete first and pairwise scalar-report moments are
insufficient in the registered heterogeneous class; the claim does not extend to
every source model or higher-order observable law.
