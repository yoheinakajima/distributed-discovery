# DD-C-0016 — information-frontier monotonicity

Assume every protocol feasible under \(I_1\) can be emulated under \(I_2\) without changing the objective or action, budget, and timing constraints. Thus \(\mathcal P_B(I_1)\) embeds in \(\mathcal P_B(I_2)\) with the same discovery value. Taking a supremum over the larger feasible class gives

\[
V_B(I_2)=\sup_{\Pi\in\mathcal P_B(I_2)}G_B(\Pi;I_2)
\ge
\sup_{\Pi\in\mathcal P_B(I_1)}G_B(\Pi;I_1)=V_B(I_1).
\]

The proposition does not compare two different implemented protocols. It is a feasible-set inclusion argument, consistent with Blackwell’s value-of-information lineage but stated only for the declared emulation relation.
