# DD-C-0011 — redundancy identity

Let \(Y_i\) indicate that action \(i\) hits the unique target, and let \(K=\sum_{i=1}^N Y_i\). With \(Q=N^{-1}\sum_i\mathbb E[Y_i]\), linearity gives \(NQ=\mathbb E[K]\). Discovery is \(G=\Pr(K\ge1)=\mathbb E[\mathbf 1\{K\ge1\}]\). Pointwise, \(K-\mathbf 1\{K\ge1\}=(K-1)^+\). Taking expectations yields \(NQ-G=\mathbb E[(K-1)^+]\). The identity counts successful actions beyond the first on discovered worlds.
