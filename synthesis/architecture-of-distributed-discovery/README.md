# The Architecture of Distributed Discovery

**Provisional subtitle:** *Foundations, Results, and Open Problems*

This directory is the scaffold for the living synthesis of the Distributed
Discovery research program. Its purpose is to define the research object,
establish common notation, explain how results fit together, distinguish
theorems from bounded results, negative results, conjectures, and open
problems, and preserve the complete intellectual story without forcing every
study into a separate paper.

The synthesis is not a new umbrella brand, a validated PDF, or an automatic
owner of theorem novelty. It cites theorem-family papers, registered studies,
claim IDs, and immutable evidence rather than silently re-owning their claims.

Provisional parts are Foundations; Canonical phenomena; Institutions and
action technologies; North-Star theory; and Open problems and measurement.
See the machine-readable chapter, claim, and source maps before adding prose.
The `maturity-map.yml` records theorem gates and evidence maturity; the
`prospectus.md` is the current long-form editorial plan, not a validated paper.
