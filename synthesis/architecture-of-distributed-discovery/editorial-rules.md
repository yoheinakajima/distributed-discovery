# Editorial rules

1. Preserve Distributed Discovery as the research-program name and use
   “discovery architecture” only as the common-noun formal object.
2. Reserve *The Architecture of Distributed Discovery* for this living
   synthesis, a future monograph or survey, or a major talk or book.
3. Cite theorem papers and registered studies; do not silently transfer
   novelty or primary claim ownership to the synthesis.
4. Attach a claim ID and source study to every scientific result. Attach an
   immutable run ID to every generated numerical result.
5. Label definitions, identities, analytic theorems, bounded exact results,
   independent reproductions, estimates, negative results, conjectures, open
   questions, and editorial recommendations distinctly.
6. Preserve failed searches, nulls, counterexamples, and scope boundaries.
7. Open theorem targets remain questions, not promotional claims.
8. Keep common notation consistent with the source model; do not hide changes
   to priors, channels, dependence, action technology, authority, rewards, or
   equilibrium selection.
9. A synthesis chapter may support several papers, but each theorem-family
   claim has one declared primary owner.
10. No validated PDF, submission, release, DOI, or peer-review representation
    is implied by this scaffold.
