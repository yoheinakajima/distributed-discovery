# Distributed Discovery brand and language system

This is a presentation system for the public research library. It does not
rename the repository, research program, studies, claims, papers,
DiscoveryBench, the Shared Discovery Paradox, or the Common-Source Trap.

## Adopted hierarchy

| Role | Language |
|---|---|
| Research program | Distributed Discovery |
| Short descriptor | How groups turn evidence into portfolios of action. |
| Academic bridge | Collective search under dispersed information. |
| Formal object | a discovery architecture |
| Core distinction | Information aggregation is not action allocation. |
| Public thesis | Collective intelligence is not sufficient for collective discovery. |
| Primary hook | Better shared information can produce worse collective discovery. |
| Design principle | Share the evidence. Diversify the actions. |
| Synthesis phrase | The Architecture of Distributed Discovery |

Use “discovery architecture” as a common noun for a formal system or
institutional design. Reserve “The Architecture of Distributed Discovery” for
a synthesis section, talk, or future long-form title. Do not present either as a
renamed program or a newly owned field.

## Language to use

- Start with the concrete tension: a group can improve its best guess and still
  waste attempts by sending everyone to the same place.
- Use evidence, sharing, actions, roles, incentives, timing, coverage, and
  discovery before introducing registries, manifests, protocols, or claim IDs.
- Use “checked two ways,” “finite study,” “synthetic only,” and “open question”
  as human status labels; retain formal status in technical details.
- Use Papers in navigation, Labs for interactive explainers, Research for the
  study catalogue, Results for findings, and Resources for technical utilities.
- Say “exact in this model” or “within the registered finite class” when scope is
  material; never let compact language broaden a result.

## Language to avoid

- Do not lead with “A Research Program in Distributed Discovery,” “Beyond the
  Paradox,” “exact and bounded results,” “passing immutable runs,” “evidence
  boundary,” “golden tasks,” “protocol contracts,” “metric registry,” “exact
  result vectors,” or raw machine statuses.
- Do not call Discovery Architecture the umbrella brand.
- Do not imply a new field, universal law, completed human experiment, hosted
  benchmark, leaderboard, submission system, DOI, peer review, or empirical
  validation.
- Do not invent proprietary paradox, trap, theorem, or law names for ordinary
  results.

## Homepage copy

- Brand/H1: **Distributed Discovery**
- Tagline: **How groups turn evidence into portfolios of action.**
- Hook: **Better shared information can produce worse collective discovery.**
- Explanation: **A group can improve its best guess and still waste its attempts
  by sending everyone to the same place. This project studies how evidence,
  roles, incentives, and timing shape a portfolio of search actions.**
- Primary CTA: **See the paradox**
- Secondary CTA: **Explore the research**
- Tertiary link: **Browse the Labs**
- Design-principle line: **Share the evidence. Diversify the actions.**

## Metadata and social copy

- Homepage title: **Distributed Discovery**
- Homepage meta description: **How groups and multi-agent systems turn evidence
  into portfolios of action.**
- Social description: **Better shared information does not guarantee better
  collective discovery. The outcome depends on how evidence is acquired,
  shared, and spread across multiple attempts.**
- Social-card copy: **Distributed Discovery — Share the evidence. Diversify the
  actions.**

## Short-form copy

- Short bio: **Distributed Discovery studies how groups and multi-agent systems
  turn dispersed evidence into portfolios of search actions—and why better
  shared information can still produce worse collective discovery.**
- Talk opener: **A team can agree on its best lead and become worse at searching.
  The problem is not only what the group knows; it is how that belief is spread
  across its attempts.**
- Academic opener: **Distributed Discovery studies collective search under
  dispersed information by separating information aggregation from action
  allocation within a discovery architecture.**

## Navigation labels

Footer/resources: Foundations, Applications, Claims, Evidence, Benchmark,
Experiment Kit, Ideas, Repository.
