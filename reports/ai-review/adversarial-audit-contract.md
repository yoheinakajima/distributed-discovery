# Adversarial internal audit contract

An adversarial audit attempts to falsify a scoped artifact. It checks missing
assumptions, invalid quantifiers, boundary cases, classical reductions,
counterexamples, alternative equilibria, evidence overpromotion, literature
collisions, corruptible certificates, provenance gaps, and editorial
ownership.

The auditor returns accepted, rejected, disputed, or blocked for each
predeclared check, with evidence paths and a minimal reproducer where
applicable. Disagreement is never collapsed into a consensus label. A failed
counterexample search remains a bounded negative search, not proof.

No adversarial audit is run by this architecture milestone, and no future
internal audit may be labeled external review or peer review.
