# Post-V5 next-program gate

Date: `2026-07-22` (America/Los_Angeles)
Evidence class: editorial recommendation informed by existing claims and a
bounded literature audit; no study, theorem, proof, run, or novelty claim.

## Decision

**Outcome A — recommend one bounded Decentralized Recovery and Equilibrium
Robustness bridge before Reliable Discovery.** This is a descriptive working
label, not a final Program V6 title. It allocates no study ID and authorizes no
research execution.

In plain language, the current paper's nearest unresolved problem is not
whether a planner can diversify actions or whether one selected equilibrium
can make sharing helpful. Those are already separated by DD-021 and DD-022.
The nearest problem is whether autonomous agents reliably choose the recovery
portfolio, and what minimal institution changes the answer.

Technically, DD-C-0098 supplies a centralized posterior top-`L` comparator,
DD-C-0106 supplies one positive selected comparison, DD-C-0109 rejects an
every-equilibrium interpretation, and DD-C-0110 preserves a strict selected-
equilibrium gap to the centralized value. That sequence makes equilibrium
robustness the direct missing arrow in the theorem spine.

## Future registration prospectus — not executed

### Central question and comparison

When does sharing lead autonomous agents to implement the same target coverage
as a centralized posterior top-`L` selector, and how does the answer change
across all, best, worst, refined, or dynamically selected equilibria?

The formal comparison must report, for the same finite channel and action
technology:

- the named direct-private discovery baseline `P_N(W)`;
- the centralized pooled value `V_L^(N)(W)` from the DD-021 interface;
- best- and worst-equilibrium decentralized pooled discovery;
- any declared refinement or dynamic selection; and
- a recovery gap or ratio whose denominator and zero conventions are frozen.

### Smallest viable model and minimal institution

Start with `M=3` targets and `N=2` agents, a finite rational channel inducing a
public posterior over targets, one reliable one-hit action per agent, and the
DD-022 equal-split unit prize. The baseline game is simultaneous, autonomous,
identity-aware, and has hidden contemporaneous actions, no assignment, no
roles, no transfers, no public random seed, and no post-disclosure messages.

Add exactly one institutional primitive: **sequential arrival with visible
prior occupancy**. The second agent observes the first action before choosing.
This is not called coordination-free; it introduces action observability and
timing, but not centralized authority. Licenses, markets, transfers,
correlated recommendations, and reliability are excluded from the first model.

### Equilibrium scopes

The registration must enumerate the complete pure simultaneous correspondence
and the complete pure subgame-perfect correspondence under visible sequential
arrival. It may add one named refinement or perturbed best-response dynamic
only if the literature and state-space audits show that doing so is necessary
for a falsifiable distinction. “The equilibrium” is prohibited under
multiplicity.

### Targets and witnesses

- **Positive theorem target:** necessary and sufficient posterior conditions
  under which every declared sequential equilibrium covers the centralized
  top-two target set, first pointwise in the public posterior and then averaged
  over the frozen channel.
- **Impossibility/lower-bound target:** a condition under which visibility and
  sequential choice still cannot guarantee centralized recovery, or a bound on
  worst-equilibrium discovery relative to `V_2`.
- **Tightness target:** match the positive boundary with equality and
  immediately adjacent rational posterior witnesses.
- **Minimal failure witness:** begin with the rational `M=3,N=2` case where
  joining the highest-posterior target pays more than taking the second target;
  prove minimality only under a predeclared order.

These are targets, not results. No inequality is promoted until proof and
verification satisfy the claim-status policy.

### Verification, corruption, and resource gate

Before any run, the future ExecPlan must:

1. derive the best-response and subgame-perfect conditions symbolically;
2. implement a direct target/signal/action/deviation enumerator;
3. implement a materially independent normal-form/backward-induction verifier;
4. compare every equilibrium's discovery, payoff, occupancy, and centralized
   gap using exact rational arithmetic;
5. corrupt posterior normalization, a deviation payoff, an equilibrium label,
   a tie boundary, the centralized comparator, and a recovery classification;
6. count target states, signal profiles, posterior classes, strategies,
   histories, deviations, and certificate rows before execution; and
7. freeze runtime and memory estimates and hard caps.

### Literature and non-overlap boundary

The registration must explicitly reduce the baseline to the closest singleton
congestion/resource-selection and multiple-searcher games in
[`post-v5-literature-and-nonoverlap.md`](post-v5-literature-and-nonoverlap.md).
Pure-equilibrium existence, a Rosenthal/Monderer–Shapley potential, generic
price of anarchy, symmetric box-search equilibrium, or standard sequential
best response is not a Distributed Discovery result. The surviving statement
must jointly involve the private-information baseline, posterior top-`L`
recovery, and a robust implementation or impossibility boundary.

### Exact stop conditions

Stop the bridge after the first of:

1. a verified necessary-and-sufficient recovery theorem with tight failure
   witness and independent certificate;
2. a sharp impossibility or lower bound with a minimal certified witness;
3. a bounded exact null that exhausts the registered class; or
4. a documented non-overlap failure showing that every proposed statement is
   already a classical special case and no information-conditioned theorem
   survives.

Do not add parameters to evade a null or overlap failure. A later paper is
considered only after independent admission; the registration itself promises
no paper.

## Relationships to completed and later work

- **DD-002:** preserves the lesson that selection can move differently from
  the planner under information refinement; the bridge does not reopen its
  disclosure census.
- **DD-017:** reuses its discipline of separating weak Nash, pairwise, larger-
  coalition, and mixed concepts; none are treated as interchangeable.
- **DD-018:** separates authoritative assignment from equilibrium
  implementation and does not infer truthfulness or coalition-proofness.
- **DD-021:** takes centralized `V_L` and recovery as the named upper
  comparator without modifying its theorem or run.
- **DD-022:** takes the selected positive interval, every-equilibrium failure,
  and centralized gap as the motivation, not as a result to rerun.
- **Reliable Discovery:** follows the bridge and changes the action technology
  through failure, redundancy, overlap, or repetition. The bridge keeps actions
  reliable and varies implementation.
- **Price of Missing Provenance:** remains separate because it varies what is
  known about dependence. The bridge initially treats its channel law as known.

## Publication and synthesis role

The bridge would fill the open arrow between the Information Sharing Frontier
paper and the Discovery Implementation family. A positive, negative, or
overlap-failure result belongs first to its registered study and then to the
living synthesis. It does not automatically extend the current paper or create
a new one.

No study ID is allocated because the model reduction, exact comparison,
literature non-overlap statement, resource counts, independent verifier,
corruptions, and issue/ExecPlan do not yet exist. This gate recommends the next
registration question; it does not perform registration.
