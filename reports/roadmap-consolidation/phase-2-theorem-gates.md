# Phase 2 theorem gates

These are unregistered questions, not queued results.

## 1. Strategic utility-spine characterization

Central question: under what information, action-space, observability, budget,
participation, and discrete-convexity conditions do equal-share,
sole-rescue, or marginal-contribution rewards align discovery portfolios?

The scope is the strategic implementation spine: acquisition, attention,
disclosure, and action-selection games with coverage value, duplicated action,
and planner-versus-equilibrium comparisons. It does not absorb signal
geometry, threshold complementarity, reliability, correlated failure,
communication limits, dynamic adaptation, or large-team scaling. The closest
literature includes valid-utility and welfare-sharing games, distributed
resource allocation, potential and congestion games, and price-of-anarchy and
price-of-stability analysis. Registration requires a minimum model map across
DD-006/DD-006B, DD-012, DD-018, and the issue #162 stop, plus a non-overlap
witness showing why the selected discovery object is not already a classical
utility-design result.

Permitted stops are an exact characterization, a scoped impossibility, an
efficiency bound, a bounded null, a minimal counterexample, or a
classical-overlap stop. Marginal-contribution closure is not a premise, and no
paper gate opens unless the model map and literature collision audit pass.

## 2. Information–Action Decomposition and Decentralized Separation Failure

Central question: when can a centralized planner emulate old policies under
better information, and when do decentralized incentives, destroyed
differentiation, or unavailable coordination prevent implementation?

The minimum model map must separately freeze the information structure, action
authority, policy-emulation class, observability, and payoff game. The closest
literature includes Blackwell comparison, team decision theory, information
design, and decentralized implementation. A required non-overlap witness must
separate a centralized emulation statement from a decentralized
equilibrium-selection failure.

Permitted stops are a centralized decomposition theorem, a scoped
decentralized impossibility, a bounded counterexample or null, or a
classical-overlap stop. It is prohibited to claim that better information
lowers a fixed centralized frontier when old policies remain emulable.

## 3. Discovery Order candidate

Central question: can information structures be compared for portfolio
discovery once the decision domain and comparison relation are fully fixed?

Before registration, the minimum model map must freeze priors, target spaces,
team sizes, action budgets, technologies, authority, protocol classes, payoff
games, exact versus approximate comparison, reflexivity, transitivity, and the
relationship to Blackwell comparison, Bergemann–Morris individual
sufficiency, and any Lehmann or Le Cam deficiency interpretation. A
non-overlap witness must show why the proposed comparison is not an existing
information order under renamed primitives.

Permitted stops are a preorder or partial-order result, an impossibility or
nontransitivity witness, a bounded null, or a classical-overlap stop. No
notation such as `>=_{disc,L}` may be normalized before those gates pass.

Each gate may stop positively, impossibly, with a bound, bounded null,
counterexample, or classical-overlap finding. None has an issue, study ID,
branch, paper promise, or registration because the model and literature gates
are not yet passed.
