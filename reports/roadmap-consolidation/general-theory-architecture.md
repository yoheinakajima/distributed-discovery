# General-theory architecture

Status: internal long-horizon synthesis; not a theorem, paper, active research
program, novelty claim, or public prize objective.

## Nucleus

Information quality and action-portfolio quality are distinct resources. The
value of an information architecture depends on the beliefs it produces, the
portfolio capacity available, the action correlation it induces, and the
implementation constraints. Existing project identities and bounded theorems
motivate this nucleus but do not establish a universal order or
representation.

## 1. Information-action frontier

Central questions: Which information structures improve top-\(L\) discovery
profiles? When can a centralized planner emulate an old policy? When do
decentralized incentives or limited authority break that monotonicity?

Owned inputs include the canonical accounting distinction, DD-019 channel
profiles, DD-020 aggregation/rescue identity, DD-021 recovery profiles, and
DD-022's selection-specific positive and every-equilibrium negative results.
Missing machinery includes a domain for information comparison, bridge
theorems separating centralized emulation from decentralized implementation,
and precise connections to Blackwell comparison, team theory, individual
sufficiency, and deficiency. Valid stops are classical reduction, scoped
counterexample, bounded null, or continued deferral.

## 2. Discovery mechanism design

Central questions: Which reporting, action, reward, assignment, observability,
and authority rules implement high-value portfolios? Which constraints make
truth, obedience, participation, differentiation, and budget balance
compatible?

DD-002, DD-006/DD-006B, DD-013, and DD-018 own bounded exact inputs. A general
characterization, impossibility boundary, and minimal observability language
are missing. Classical covering games, potential/congestion games, distributed
welfare design, information design, and implementation theory are mandatory
collision checks. The issue-162 stop shows that a classical-overlap outcome is
successful and must remain available.

## 3. Adaptive and dynamic discovery

Central questions: How should evidence, action, feedback, communication,
stopping, and repetition change over time? When is redundancy valuable because
execution is unreliable or failures are correlated?

DD-004 and DD-015 provide sequential bounded evidence; DD-005 supplies
overlapping coverage; Reliable Discovery remains held. Missing bridges include
a declared feedback filtration, adaptive value object, failure law,
reliability-adjusted recovery budget, and finite theorem before any asymptotic
claim. Classical sequential search, adaptive submodularity, bandits, reliability
allocation, and distributed control are collision risks.

## 4. Algorithms and computational complexity

Central questions: Which discovery-architecture problems admit exact dynamic
programs, symmetry reductions, approximation guarantees, certificates, or
hardness results? Which input representations make those statements
meaningful?

The repository owns several exact enumerators, independent orbit/histogram
methods, policy-signature reductions, and finite registries. Those are
reproducibility assets, not a complexity theory. Missing pieces are explicit
input encodings, size measures, decision/optimization formulations, lower
bounds, approximation regimes, and reusable algorithms without DD identifiers.

## Field-defining-theory criteria

A future general theory should supply reusable objects, bridge theorems,
structural classifications, algorithms, machinery usable without project
study IDs, and precise classical-overlap boundaries. Aspirational prize
language is translated into these internal criteria and is not a public
objective.

## Why this is not active

Phase 2 freezes theorem execution. The benchmark evidence base is not
claim-grade, Reliable Discovery is held, several bridges may reduce to
classical theory, and no single bounded gate currently owns the four-part
agenda. Review after claim-grade Agents v1 evidence, the first stable
compendium release, or an explicit owner decision reopening theorem work.
