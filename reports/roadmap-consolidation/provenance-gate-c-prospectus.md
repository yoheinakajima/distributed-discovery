# Provenance Gate C prospectus

Status: gated candidate, not a study, claim, run, theorem, result, or public
coinage.

The candidate asks what discovery value is identified or guaranteed when
source provenance or dependence is only partially observed. The working
internal label “Price of Missing Provenance” remains prohibited on public
result surfaces until a separate gate establishes a theorem or certified
boundary.

## Frozen dimensions for a future gate

- observable signal, source, and dependence summaries;
- latent dependence class;
- provenance-aware architecture;
- provenance-blind architecture;
- action budget and discovery objective;
- identification region and its sharpness convention;
- worst-case additive discovery regret;
- minimax regret;
- ratio only on domains bounded away from zero with a declared denominator
  floor; and
- value of an additional provenance observable.

Worst-case additive discovery regret is the default candidate because blind
discovery may be zero. A ratio is secondary and undefined outside its
denominator-safe domain.

## Mandatory reductions

The gate must compare the object with the Manski/partial-identification line,
robust decision and minimax regret, Blackwell comparison, source/dependence
identification, and existing price-of-X quantities. It stops if no
discovery-specific object survives those reductions. A positive gate must
state observables, latent class, sharpness, loss convention, authority,
baselines, and non-equivalence witness before allocating a study.

No study ID, claim, run, issue, branch, public quantity, or theorem promise is
authorized.
