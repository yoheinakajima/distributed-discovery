## Current disposition

*Threshold Discovery* remains a validated 20-page public working paper. Its
deterministic minimum-viable-team theorem, exact threshold census, equilibrium
selection results, dynamic planner extension, and bounded mechanism evidence
remain valid at their recorded scopes (DD-C-0071 through DD-C-0086). DD-020

The paper's formal admission/submission decision remains held behind the
**Reliable Discovery and Action Technology** theorem gate. No manuscript,
submission, release, DOI, or journal action is taken now.

## Material strengthening required

Reliable Discovery would materially strengthen or absorb the paper only if it
produces a general finite result with all of the following declared:

1. candidate posterior masses;
2. stochastic action or team success probabilities;
3. dependence or independence of execution failures;
4. replication, overlap, and threshold team technology;
5. the planner's physical action budget;
6. an exact characterization, certified bound, or minimal counterexample for
   when the optimum diversifies, repeats, overlaps, or forms minimum teams.

The strongest useful target is a reliability-aware allocation theorem that
recovers DD-C-0071 as the perfect-execution threshold special case and yields
reliability-adjusted recovery budgets. Another finite catalogue without a
title-level structural result is insufficient to absorb the paper.

## Stopping boundary

Register at most one bounded Reliable Discovery study at the next authorized
theorem gate. After that study:

- if a general theorem materially changes the title-level threshold result,
  reassess integration and primary ownership;
- if the result is a minimal counterexample that sharply limits such a
  theorem, incorporate the boundary and judge the existing paper on its own;
- if only bounded classifications or a null search are found, preserve them
  publicly, stop the hold, and judge *Threshold Discovery* independently under
  the five-part paper-admission rule.

This stopping rule prevents an indefinite calendar hold. The gate is resolved
by a theorem, a sharp counterexample, or one declared failed bounded attempt—not
by repeated parameter expansion.
