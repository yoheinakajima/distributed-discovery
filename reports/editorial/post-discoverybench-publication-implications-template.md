# Post-DiscoveryBench publication-implications template

Use this template only after a separately registered, owner-authorized campaign
produces validated evidence with immutable provenance. The completed public
credential preflight and calibration do not activate it.

## Paper implications

- Which existing papers are supported, complicated, contradicted, or untouched?
- What exact validated result and run ID supports every classification?
- Does a contradiction target a theorem, a behavioral interpretation, or only
  one software-agent protocol?

## Publication architecture

- Is the contribution best owned by a standalone benchmark paper?
- Would a grand sequel add a genuine theorem family, or merely repackage
  existing owners?
- What is the smallest editorial unit that can state the result without
  re-owning prior claims?

## Technical companions

Record whether the publication needs a benchmark specification, evaluator and
provenance note, contamination/custody note, model-route and cost appendix, or
replication package. A companion is not a substitute for evidence validation.

## Absorption and lifecycle

For each existing paper, record exact text or figure implications without
editing it until a paper-specific lane is authorized. Check claim validation,
immutable runs, preserved nulls and failures, primary literature, ownership,
replication status, privacy closeout, cost reconciliation, paper build, visual
QA, and explicit submission or release approval.

## Collision, nulls, and limits

Name the closest primary neighbors, overlap, surviving boundary, unresolved
source checks, headline nulls, negative results, failed routes, and forbidden
generalizations. Novelty language remains disallowed until independently
supported.

Current disposition: `hold`. Public calibration authorizes no paper action.
