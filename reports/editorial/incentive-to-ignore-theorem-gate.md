# Editorial theorem gate — The Incentive to Ignore

Date: `2026-07-22`  
Evidence admitted: DD-012 through DD-014 and DD-020  
Decision type: editorial recommendation, not submission authority

## Decision

The Incremental Sharing gate is **met**. DD-020 produced a general finite
point-channel theorem, not merely a bounded classification: under its declared
one-action block protocol, each additional pooled searcher weakly lowers group
discovery, strictly unless signals are perfect (DD-C-0094). The result
materially expands the program beyond DD-012's one-reader model, but it should
be **owned primarily by the Information Sharing, Attention, and Recovery
theorem family**. *The Incentive to Ignore* should cite it as a companion
result and retain its existing title-level ownership of strategic attention,
audience design, and the One-Reader Theorem.

Editorial disposition: **cite as a companion result**. The working paper no
longer needs to be held for the Incremental Sharing gate. It now qualifies for
independent paper-admission review as a self-contained theorem-family paper;
no submission, release, DOI, journal contact, or manuscript rewrite is
authorized here.

## Gate questions

### 1. Does DD-020 materially expand beyond the one-reader model?

Yes. DD-012 assumes one exogenous shared clue of accuracy `q`; every attending
role follows that same clue, while nonattenders retain independent private
point actions. Its One-Reader Theorem identifies the socially optimal number
of shared-clue readers in the registered follow/private class (DD-C-0059), and
its equal-split analysis supplies strategic attention thresholds
(DD-C-0060–0061).

DD-020 instead lets the sharing block pool `s` private point signals and
recompute one posterior-MAP action. It proves the aggregation–rescue identity
(DD-C-0092), the two-agent decline (DD-C-0093), and the general finite
point-channel ordering (DD-C-0094). Aggregation quality therefore changes with
block size rather than remaining an exogenous `q`. That is a material theorem,
not a parameter extension of DD-012.

### 2. Is it the same signal and action architecture?

It is adjacent, not identical. Both use finite uniform targets,
conditionally independent symmetric point signals, simultaneous one-hit
actions, one duplicated common action for the sharing group, and independent
private actions outside that group. Both evaluate union discovery.

The differences matter. DD-012's shared clue is exogenous; DD-020's common
recommendation is the MAP output of pooled private signals. DD-012 fixes three
targets in its census and studies access rules, equal-split payoffs, and
equilibrium; DD-020 proves a nonstrategic common-objective result for arbitrary
finite `M,N` and then supplies a bounded arbitrary-channel counterexample.
Calling them the same model would erase the theorem's mechanism.

### 3. Does it change the paper's title-level result?

No. The paper's title-level result is about incentives and selective access:
social discovery can prefer one reader while private equal-split incentives
select too much or too little attention. DD-020 has no reward or equilibrium
choice. It strengthens the broader intellectual case for preserving
independent action, but it does not replace the strategic attention theorem.

### 4. Would incorporation require structural rewriting?

Yes, if incorporated as a co-owned theorem. A full integration would require a
second model section, new aggregation notation `C_s`, a separate MAP
containment proof, the uninformative-boundary treatment, a larger literature
position around jury accuracy and list/set decisions, and a careful separation
of strategic access from nonstrategic pooling. That would broaden the title,
abstract, proof sequence, and referee set. A concise companion citation and
scope paragraph require no such rewrite.

### 5. Who should own the result?

- **Primary owner:** Information Sharing, Attention, and Recovery theorem
  family; future Information Sharing Frontier paper.
- **Supporting relationship:** *The Incentive to Ignore* may cite DD-020 as a
  companion theorem showing that the value of leaving nonreaders is not an
  artifact of a fixed shared clue.
- **Study/evidence owner:** DD-020 and claims DD-C-0092 through DD-C-0096.
- **Synthesis role:** Chapter “Information Sharing Frontier” explains the
  relationship without taking novelty ownership.

Primary ownership is intentionally not duplicated. DD-012 continues to own
DD-C-0059 through DD-C-0061; DD-020 owns DD-C-0092 through DD-C-0096.

### 6. Has the declared hold gate been met?

Yes. The hold required a proposition materially beyond the one-reader model.
DD-C-0094 supplies a general finite point-channel theorem and DD-C-0096 shows
why that theorem cannot be exported to arbitrary channels from one-person
accuracy alone. The gate is therefore closed with a positive theorem and a
scope-defining counterexample.

public working paper; no external publication action is authorized.
