# Paper citation and ownership graph

The canonical upstream paper owns the atomic information/action paradox.
Common-Source Trap owns endogenous common-source acquisition; Incentive to
Ignore owns selective attention and audience design; Threshold Discovery owns
minimum viable teams; Information Sharing Frontier owns signal geometry,
independent rescue, recovery budgets, and its selected positive-sharing
boundary.

Incentive and Frontier cite each other as companions without merging ownership.
Threshold is positioned against coverage, public-good, congestion, and
implementation work. Common-Source is positioned against parallel search,
R&D, diversity, organizational design, and endogenous acquisition. Syntheses
point readers to primary owners rather than silently re-owning results.

The graph in `paper-citation-graph.yml` records only relationships that can be
explained. Citation count is not a quality target.
