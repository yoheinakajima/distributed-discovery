# Paper recomposition prospectus

The current paper set has meaningful overlap but no authorized lifecycle
transition. The canonical anchor is locked. Incentive to Ignore and
Information Sharing Frontier have distinct central questions and should remain
separate. Common-Source Trap may later be absorbed by a successful provenance
paper, and Threshold Discovery may later be absorbed by Reliable Discovery,
but neither replacement exists.

Foundations, Three Results, and Discovery Institutions are plausible future
demotion or merger candidates only after an accepted living-synthesis or
strategic-spine replacement fully absorbs their roles and preserves stable
artifacts and citations. Today they remain useful independent notes.

The principal recomposition risk is editorial overreach: chronological
copying, hidden ownership transfer, lost counterboundaries, or archive before
replacement acceptance. The machine-readable map therefore records a
no-action rationale and prerequisite for every paper. This prospectus executes
no merge, split, absorption, demotion, retirement, archive, withdrawal, or
supersession.
