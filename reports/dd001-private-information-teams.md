# DD-001 — Private-information teams initial results

The initial zero-communication study is complete enough to establish exact tiny cases and a bounded canonical lower bound, but not the canonical optimum.

## Main result

Direct clue-following is not generally team-optimal, even with informative independent clues and fewer searchers than candidate locations. Exhaustive rational enumeration certifies the \(M=3,N=2,p=2/5\) optimum as \(7/10\), versus direct value \(16/25\). A hybrid role profile gains \(3/50\): one searcher holds a fixed territory while the other follows a signal-contingent partial territory. The primary run certifies three additional informative \(N<M\) counterexamples.

## Evidence distinctions

- Tiny-grid optima: exact computational results with exhaustive completeness counts, exact rational evaluation, and an independent direct enumerator.
- Deterministic sufficiency and benchmark inequalities: derived propositions with local proofs.
- Canonical direct value: exact constructive lower bound.
- Canonical coordinate fixed point and restart convergence: checked numerical observations.
- Canonical private-team optimum: unresolved.
