## 11. Classical-reduction strategy

Map players, resources, occupancy, payoff, social discovery, potential, and
equilibrium correspondence exactly. Separate Nash existence from full
implementation. Compare one round, public posterior, visibility, collision
reward, and robustness against Korman–Rodeh. Treat backward induction and
subgame perfection as classical. A surviving statement must require the
private baseline, public posterior, centralized comparator, autonomous game,
equilibrium scope, and an exact recovery/failure boundary in a way not supplied
by this reduction.

## 12. Candidate model

Three targets and two identity-aware agents share an exact public posterior.
Each chooses one reliable target action. If `n_j` agents choose the true target
`j`, each receives `1/n_j`. The simultaneous form hides contemporaneous
actions. In the sequential form Agent 2 observes Agent 1's action. No other
institution is present.

posterior, enumerates every action/deviation and complete contingent strategy,
then performs backward induction without importing Method A's region labels.
