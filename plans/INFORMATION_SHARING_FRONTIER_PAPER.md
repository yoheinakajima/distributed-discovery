# Information Sharing Frontier paper execution plan

This is a living ExecPlan for the admitted candidate *When Does Information
Sharing Improve Decentralized Discovery?*. It is created by documentation-only
gate issue #152 and associated with paper issue #153.

## Purpose and intended outcome

If a later explicit task authorizes manuscript execution, build a
self-contained archival theorem paper that explains when information sharing
helps or harms discovery through signal geometry, aggregation, independent
rescue, action authority, congestion, and equilibrium selection. The paper must
make DD-022's selected decentralized theorem and its every-equilibrium failure
equally visible.

## Current state

The candidate is admitted for planning only. DD-019 through DD-022 are merged,
deployed, and owned by the Information Sharing Frontier family. Claims
DD-C-0089 through DD-C-0110 and their immutable runs are the complete authorized
evidence base. No manuscript source, generated asset, PDF, submission, release,
DOI, or journal action exists or is authorized by this plan.

## Scope

The paper's central question is: when does sharing useful private information
improve rather than reduce decentralized group discovery under a fully declared
information channel, action budget, authority structure, reward, and equilibrium
selection?

The proposed title-level theorem is DD-C-0106, supported by DD-C-0104,
DD-C-0105, and DD-C-0107. DD-C-0109 is a coequal limitation. DD-019 through
DD-021 supply the signal-geometry, aggregation/rescue, general-frontier, and
centralized-comparator layers.

## Non-goals

Do not claim every-equilibrium improvement, coalition-proofness, arbitrary
source laws, arbitrary targets or team sizes, planner implementation, empirical
validity, human behavior, verified novelty, or journal fit without new evidence
or review. Do not rerun immutable configurations. Do not absorb the strategic-
attention theorem owned by *The Incentive to Ignore*.

   record search dates, map the exact contribution boundary, and stop any claim

   generated-evidence interfaces following the theorem/section map. Freeze the
   abstract claim before prose expansion.
3. **Theorem integration.** Re-state DD-019 through DD-022 results with exact
   source claim IDs, declared authority, selected-equilibrium scope, and no
   evidence-status promotion.
4. **Failure and selection boundary.** Present DD-C-0095, DD-C-0103, and
   DD-C-0109 as substantive counterboundaries, including private discovery-one
   equilibria and ownership-aware disagreement splitting.
5. **Generated assets and provenance.** Generate every table and figure from
   immutable result artifacts, validate source hashes, and avoid manual numeric
   transcription.
6. **Independent paper audit.** Validate equations, citations, cross-references,
   claim ownership, terminology, theorem hypotheses, and all generated assets.
7. **Build and visual QA.** Rebuild deterministically, render every page, inspect

   verification.
8. **Editorial review only.** Open a paper PR and stop at a validated working
   paper. Submission, release, DOI, and journal contact require separate owner
   authorization.
