## Source-of-truth boundary

editorial decisions, and roadmap decisions. Existing Python and Make workflows
remain authoritative for validation and deterministic builds. The new
editorial registries cannot create evidence, alter claim status, or supersede
the claim ledger. ActiveGraph is advisory and out of scope.

## AI-only boundary

The architecture distinguishes producing research, context-isolated
reproduction, materially distinct-method verification, adversarial proof,
classical reduction, primary-source literature, corruption, editorial
boundary, integration, and final acceptance roles. Role definitions are
prospective internal controls. No AI audit in this repository is peer review,
external review, independent scholarly validation, community acceptance, or
outside reproduction.

## Optionality design

Every option records readiness, residual value, decay, estimated expiry,
trigger, first irreversible spend, kill/defer condition, required evidence,
AI-native validation, dependencies, current authority, prohibited actions,
visibility, and next review. Values are qualitative or broad intervals.
DiscoveryBench Agents is first to exercise; all others remain design or
theorem-gate options. Stopped options remain recorded and no simultaneous
execution branches are authorized.

## Translation architecture

One authoritative concordance maps canonical objects to OR/MS, economics/AGT,
multi-agent AI, and practitioner formulations. Relation types distinguish
exact equivalents, standard-literature neighbors, approximate restatements,
analogies, and practitioner formulations. Each record resolves supporting
claims, studies, paper owners, and literature adjacency; includes mandatory
qualifiers and prohibited phrasing; and names cleared public surfaces.
`claims/claims.yml` is unchanged.

## Paper lifecycle architecture

Scientific, editorial, and publication status are independent. Lifecycle
records freeze routes, downloads, checksums, page counts, ownership,
counterboundaries, citation instructions, build policy, and upstream lock.
The unique upstream anchor is canonical-published in the owner-declared sense
qualified above. Four local flagships are active working papers; Foundations
is an active research note; Three Results and Discovery Institutions are
active synthesis notes. No predecessor status changes in this milestone.

## Recomposition workflow

A future merge, split, absorb, demote, retirement, or supersession begins from
a central question and claim selection, assigns roles and primary ownership,
audits scientific equivalence/literature/counterboundaries, builds and accepts
a replacement, and only then changes predecessor lifecycle. Stable routes,
PDFs, hashes, and citation instructions remain available.
